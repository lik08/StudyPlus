StudyPlus - The Future of Learning

The purpose of StudyPlus is to be engaging and enjoyable for students and self-learners. It has been designed to enhance the learning experience by providing them resources to improve their understanding and knowledge retention of topics they are studying.

See Demo

Vision Statement

Who are the users?

StudyPlus is designed to be engaging and enjoyable for any individual looking to broaden their knowledge about a specific topic. While StudyPlus can be used by anyone with an interest in learning, it is primarily designed for students. StudyPlus allows its learners to organize and access course material in one place. Additionally, they will be able to assess their knowledge through practice assessments.

What do we want to accomplish?

StudyPlus allows you to take your studying on the go while also giving learners a way to store their studying materials. This allows students to go back and forth between the things like practice assessments and their flash cards at their discretion. Motivation is one of the biggest issues students face throughout their academic journey. StudyPlus aims to motivate learners and reduce burnout by making studying a more fun and enjoyable experience. Another objective is to increase accessibility. Learners typically find small excuses such as having to carry around notebooks or large devices to avoid studying. StudyPlus will aim to reduce the number of barriers that students face and hence promoting studying.

Postmortem

What would you do differently, if you had the chance to start over?

If we had the chance to start over, we would put a lot more time and effort in the beginning stages of building our application. We should have spent more time developing a roadmap as well as laying out the overall architecture before actually coding and developing the application. We ran into so many problems and issues which resulted in so many pivots and changes of our plans.

The number of features and the complexities of each were also just too much for the amount of time we had. We should have built simpler features that were well developed, scale, and interact with other parts of our app fluidly.

What went wrong in the development process?

One area that could have been improved during the development process was the planning of the overall project as well as the planning before every iteration. The group struggled to keep on pace with what was discussed during the planning stages which ultimately led to a multitude of problems with work distribution, time management, and organization.

The work distribution and time management problems were a result of not understanding the scope and size of the features and associated tasks. We were too ambitious and as we continued to build our project, we ran into a lot of issues with crashes and bugs which ended up shortening the amount of time we had. This led to cutting certain functionalities of features and frantic development.

What took the most time?

Setting up the database during iteration two ended up being a lot harder than anticipated. After fiddling with our database for many hours, we eventually had it correctly implemented in our project for iteration two. As we were developing more features and functionalities for iteration three, we ran into a lot of bugs and ended up consuming a lot of time that could have been spent developing more features. In hindsight, we should have spent more time developing a better ER diagram, which would have saved us time down the road.

How did the project change?

The purpose of our project has not changed, but the number of features and functions we wanted to implement changed throughout the project. The main features that we wanted to implement and were able to was a practice assessment tool (quiz feature) and a flash card system. We also wanted a way for our users to organize their studying materials, which is done through course organization.

One feature that didn't make it in time that we hope to include in the future, is the ability to create profiles which also includes progress reports. This would have allowed our users to track their progress as well as informing them on areas of strengths and weaknesses to shift their studying accordingly.
